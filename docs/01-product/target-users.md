# Target Users

## Primary User Segments

FitGenie serves two sides of a marketplace: **customers** who want perfectly fitted clothing, and **service providers** (tailors/designers) who want digital reach.

---

## Segment 1 – End Customers

### 1A. Online Shopper (Frustrated Returner)
- **Age:** 18–40
- **Gender:** All genders
- **Behaviour:** Shops online frequently; regularly faces sizing issues and returns.
- **Pain point:** Cannot trust size charts; wastes time and money on returns.
- **Goal:** Get the right size the first time, with confidence.
- **Device:** Primarily smartphone.

### 1B. Occasion/Event Dresser
- **Age:** 22–55
- **Gender:** All genders
- **Behaviour:** Shops for weddings, festivals, or formal occasions. Invests more per purchase.
- **Pain point:** Needs a perfect, personalised fit for a specific outfit and event.
- **Goal:** Discover a tailor or designer who can deliver custom work on time.
- **Device:** Smartphone and tablet.

### 1C. Gift Buyer
- **Age:** 25–50
- **Gender:** All genders
- **Behaviour:** Shopping for a partner, family member, or friend. Does not have the recipient's measurements.
- **Pain point:** High uncertainty about fit when buying for others.
- **Goal:** Guided suggestions based on approximate details; easy return/alteration path.
- **Device:** Smartphone.

### 1D. Style Explorer (New to fashion guidance)
- **Age:** 16–30
- **Gender:** All genders
- **Behaviour:** Interested in style but lacks confidence. Relies on AI/social input.
- **Pain point:** Does not know what suits their body type or how to communicate style preferences.
- **Goal:** Receive clear, respectful guidance that builds confidence.
- **Device:** Smartphone.

---

## Segment 2 – Service Providers

### 2A. Independent Tailor
- **Age:** 25–60
- **Location:** Local neighbourhood or city-level.
- **Behaviour:** Takes custom stitching orders; relies on walk-in customers.
- **Pain point:** Limited digital visibility; measurement handoff is all manual/verbal.
- **Goal:** Get more orders digitally; receive structured fit cards to reduce errors.
- **Device:** Smartphone (basic); may need desktop for order management.

### 2B. Fashion Designer (Boutique)
- **Age:** 25–50
- **Behaviour:** Creates designer wear with a portfolio. Has an established client base but wants wider reach.
- **Pain point:** Client onboarding is slow; fit consultations are time-consuming.
- **Goal:** Showcase portfolio, attract new clients, and receive digital fit data.
- **Device:** Desktop and smartphone.

### 2C. Alteration Specialist
- **Age:** 25–55
- **Behaviour:** Specialises in alterations and adjustments to ready-made clothes.
- **Pain point:** Customers bring ill-fitting garments without structured measurements.
- **Goal:** Receive structured fit cards and alteration notes digitally.
- **Device:** Smartphone.

---

## User Persona Summaries

| Persona | Primary Need | Key Feature Used |
|---|---|---|
| Frustrated Returner | Correct size, first time | AI Fit Scanner + Confidence Score |
| Occasion Dresser | Custom fit + tailor discovery | Tailor Marketplace + Booking |
| Gift Buyer | Fit guidance for others | Profile-for-others + Smart Filters |
| Style Explorer | Style direction + confidence | AI Recommendation + "Why this suits you" |
| Independent Tailor | More orders + structured data | Fit Card + Booking Management |
| Fashion Designer | Portfolio reach + client onboarding | Designer Profile + Fit Card |
| Alteration Specialist | Structured alteration requests | Fit Card + Notes |

---

## Out of Scope Users (Phase 1)

- Enterprise/bulk clothing buyers.
- International users outside initial target markets (Phase 2+).
- Users under 13 years of age (minor compliance deferred to Phase 2).
