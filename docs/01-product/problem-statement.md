# Problem Statement

## Overview

FitGenie addresses a fundamental gap in the fashion industry: **the inability to reliably match clothing to individual body measurements** in both online retail and tailoring contexts.

---

## Core Problems

### 1. Sizing Inconsistency in Online Shopping
- Standard size labels (S, M, L, XL) vary significantly across brands and regions.
- Customers return 30–40% of online clothing purchases due to poor fit.
- No standardized mapping between body measurements and product sizing.

### 2. Disconnect Between Customers and Tailors/Designers
- Finding a qualified local tailor or designer involves guesswork.
- Sharing body measurements with tailors is manual, error-prone, and unstructured.
- No digital trail for fit preferences, past orders, or tailor communication.

### 3. Lack of Personalized Fashion Guidance
- Generic recommendation engines suggest based on purchase history, not actual body fit.
- Users lack confidence in style choices without expert guidance.
- No system captures personal style, budget, fabric preference, and occasion together.

### 4. Body Image Sensitivity
- Traditional sizing and fitting tools can reinforce harmful body-image perceptions.
- Users need a system that is inclusive, respectful, and non-judgmental in its language.

---

## Impact

| Problem | User Impact | Business Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Sizing inconsistency | Frustration, returns, lost trust | High return costs, low retention |
| Tailor disconnect | Wasted time, poor fit results | Low tailor utilisation |
| No personalization | Poor discovery, low engagement | Low conversion rates |
| Body-image insensitivity | User discomfort, drop-off | Negative brand perception |

---

## What FitGenie Does NOT Solve (Non-Goals)

- Physical garment manufacturing or warehousing.
- Real-time fabric/material quality assessment.
- Social media fashion trend analysis beyond recommendation input.
- Payment processing infrastructure (delegated to third-party gateway).

---

## Assumptions

- Users are willing to share body measurements digitally within a trusted app.
- A camera-based body scan can provide sufficient accuracy for size recommendation (within ±1 cm tolerance for primary measurements).
- Tailors and designers are willing to onboard onto a digital marketplace.
- The primary markets in Phase 1 are South Asia (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh), with English and regional language support to follow.

---

## Success Criteria

- ≥ 85% of users receive a size recommendation they find accurate after first scan.
- Return-or-alteration rate for FitGenie-matched outfits ≤ 10%.
- Average tailor booking time reduced from days to under 24 hours.
- 0 instances of harmful or body-negative language in AI-generated output.
